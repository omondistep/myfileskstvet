## 1. MEANING OF KEY TERMS

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Sole Trader** | A business owned and run by one person who bears all risks and enjoys all profits |
| **Revenue** | Income earned from the main business activity (e.g., sales) |
| **Cost of Sales (Cost of Goods Sold)** | The direct cost of goods sold during a period |
| **Gross Profit** | Revenue minus Cost of Sales |
| **Net Profit** | Gross Profit minus operating expenses |
| **Capital** | The owner's investment in the business |
| **Drawings** | Cash or goods taken by the owner for personal use |
| **Assets** | Resources owned by the business (e.g., cash, vehicles, stock) |
| **Liabilities** | Amounts owed by the business to outsiders (e.g., creditors, loans) |
| **Non-Current Assets (Fixed Assets)** | Assets held for long-term use (e.g., land, machinery, vehicles) |
| **Current Assets** | Assets expected to be converted to cash within 12 months (e.g., stock, debtors, cash) |
| **Current Liabilities** | Debts due within 12 months (e.g., creditors, bank overdraft) |
| **Non-Current Liabilities** | Debts due after more than 12 months (e.g., long-term bank loan) |
| **Working Capital** | Current Assets minus Current Liabilities |
| **Capital Expenditure** | Spending on acquiring or improving non-current assets |
| **Revenue Expenditure** | Day-to-day running expenses of the business |

## 2. CHARACTERISTICS OF A SOLE TRADER

A sole proprietorship is the simplest and most common form of business in Kenya — from mama mboga market stalls to professional consultants.

**Key Characteristics:**

1. **Owned by one person** — The owner makes all business decisions
2. **Unlimited liability** — The owner is personally liable for all business debts (personal assets like house, car can be seized)
3. **Simple to form** — No complex registration required (though a business name can be registered with the Registrar of Business Names in Kenya)
4. **Owner keeps all profits** — No sharing with partners or shareholders
5. **Not a separate legal entity** — The business and the owner are legally the same person
6. **Limited capital** — Relies on owner's personal savings and small loans
7. **No requirement to publish accounts** — Financial statements are private (but must maintain records for KRA)

**Examples in Kenya:**
- A grocery shop (duka) in Eastlands, Nairobi
- A hairdressing salon in Kisumu
- A boda boda operator in Bungoma
- A freelance IT consultant in Westlands
- A hardware store in Meru

## 3. THE INCOME STATEMENT (STATEMENT OF PROFIT OR LOSS)

### 3.1 Structure

The Income Statement has two parts:

**Part 1: Trading Account** — Calculates Gross Profit

```
Revenue (Sales)                                          XXX
Less: Sales Returns                                      (XXX)
Net Revenue                                              XXX
Less: Cost of Sales:
    Opening Stock                            XXX
    Add: Purchases                           XXX
    Add: Carriage Inwards                    XXX
    Less: Purchases Returns                 (XXX)
    Cost of Goods Available for Sale         XXX
    Less: Closing Stock                     (XXX)
    Cost of Sales                                       (XXX)
Gross Profit                                             XXX
```

**Part 2: Profit or Loss Section** — Calculates Net Profit

```
Gross Profit                                             XXX
Add: Other Income:
    Discount Received                        XXX
    Commission Received                      XXX
    Rent Received                            XXX          XXX
                                                         XXX
Less: Operating Expenses:
    Wages and Salaries                       XXX
    Rent Paid                                XXX
    Electricity                              XXX
    Insurance                                XXX
    Depreciation                             XXX
    Bad Debts                                XXX
    Discount Allowed                         XXX
    Carriage Outwards                        XXX
    General Expenses                         XXX         (XXX)
Net Profit                                                XXX
```

### 3.2 Important Notes

- **Carriage inwards** = Cost of transporting goods TO the business (included in cost of sales)
- **Carriage outwards** = Cost of delivering goods TO customers (operating expense)
- **Discount allowed** = Discount given to customers for prompt payment (expense)
- **Discount received** = Discount received from suppliers for prompt payment (income)

## 6. CAPITAL vs REVENUE EXPENDITURE

| Feature | Capital Expenditure | Revenue Expenditure |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Definition** | Spending to acquire or improve non-current assets | Spending on day-to-day running of the business |
| **Effect** | Creates or enhances an asset | Maintains existing earning capacity |
| **Period** | Benefits extend beyond one year | Benefits consumed within the accounting period |
| **Treatment** | Recorded as a non-current asset in the balance sheet | Charged as an expense in the income statement |
| **Examples** | Buying a delivery van (KES 1,500,000); Building an extension to a shop; Installing a new engine in a lorry | Fuel for the delivery van; Repainting the shop; Routine servicing of the lorry |

**Kenyan Example:**
Otieno's Transport Company in Nairobi:
- **Capital expenditure:** Purchasing a new 33-seater bus for KES 4,500,000
- **Revenue expenditure:** Paying for the bus's annual insurance (KES 85,000), fuel (KES 30,000/month), driver's salary (KES 25,000/month)

### Question 3: Discussion

Explain THREE differences between a sole trader's financial statements and those of a limited company.

**Model Answer:**

1. **Ownership section:** A sole trader has a Capital Account showing the owner's equity. A limited company has Share Capital and Reserves (retained earnings).

2. **Profit distribution:** A sole trader's net profit belongs entirely to the owner (reduced by drawings). A limited company distributes profit as dividends to shareholders, with undistributed profits going to retained earnings.

3. **Publication requirement:** A sole trader is not required to publish financial statements (they are private). A limited company in Kenya (especially a public company listed on the NSE) must publish audited financial statements as required by the Companies Act, 2015.
