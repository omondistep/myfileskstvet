## 1. THE RESEARCH TITLE

### 1.1 Length Requirement
A research proposal title **must be between 10 and 20 words** in length. This ensures the title is specific enough to communicate the study clearly without being overly verbose.

### 1.2 Three Components of a Research Title

Every research title must contain three distinct components:

| Component | Role | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Independent Variable (IV)** | The Cause | The factor the researcher manipulates or studies as the influencing element |
| **Dependent Variable (DV)** | The Problem | The outcome or effect being studied — what is affected by the IV |
| **Sector of Focus** | Area of Interest | The specific context, institution, location, or population being studied |

### 1.3 How the Components Relate

```
Independent Variable  ──────►  Dependent Variable
       (Cause)                     (Problem/Effect)
                    within
              Sector of Focus
```

The independent variable is hypothesized to **influence, affect, or predict** the dependent variable within the specified sector.

### 1.4 Title Construction Formula

> **[Independent Variable]** and **[Dependent Variable]** in **[Sector of Focus]**

### 1.5 Worked Examples

| Title | IV | DV | Sector |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commercialization and University Funding Model in Public Universities in Kenya | Commercialization | University Funding Model | Public Universities in Kenya |
| Economic Factors and Trainee Dropout in TVETs in Nairobi County | Economic Factors | Trainee Dropout | Public TVETs in Nairobi County |
| Instructor Qualifications and Quality of Training in Private TVETs in Mombasa | Instructor Qualifications | Quality of Training | Private TVETs in Mombasa |
| Digital Learning Tools and Student Performance in Secondary Schools in Kisumu | Digital Learning Tools | Student Performance | Secondary Schools in Kisumu |

### 1.6 Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Title that is too short (fewer than 10 words) — lacks specificity
- ❌ Title that is too long (more than 20 words) — becomes unwieldy
- ❌ Missing the sector of focus — the study has no grounded context
- ❌ Vague variables — e.g., "factors" without specifying what kind
- ❌ Using a question as the title — titles are declarative, not interrogative

## 2. RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

### 2.1 Types of Objectives

A research proposal contains **two levels** of objectives:

1. **General Objective** — one broad statement derived directly from the title
2. **Specific Objectives** — 3 to 4 focused statements derived from the indicators of the independent variable

> **Total objectives in a proposal: 3–4 specific objectives** (plus 1 general objective)

---

### 2.2 The General Objective

#### Rule
The general objective **flows directly from the research title**. It restates the title as a research aim using an action verb.

#### Standard Formula

> **To investigate the influence of [Independent Variable] on [Dependent Variable] in [Sector of Focus]**

#### Common Action Verbs for General Objectives

| Verb | Use Case |
|---|---|
| To investigate | Exploring relationships or influences |
| To examine | Analysing existing conditions |
| To assess | Measuring extent or degree |
| To determine | Establishing facts or conclusions |
| To establish | Confirming existence of a relationship |

#### Worked Example

| Title | General Objective |
|---|---|
| Economic Factors and Trainee Dropout in TVETs in Nairobi County | To investigate the influence of economic factors on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County |
| Commercialization and University Funding Model in Public Universities in Kenya | To examine the effect of commercialization on the university funding model in public universities in Kenya |

---

### 2.3 Specific Objectives

#### Rule
Specific objectives are derived from the **indicators (sub-variables) of the independent variable**, as identified from **literature review** (past studies). Each specific objective addresses one indicator of the IV.

#### How to Identify Indicators

When you conduct a literature review on your independent variable, past studies will reveal the specific dimensions or sub-components of that variable. These become your indicators.

**Example:**

> **Independent Variable:** Economic Factors  
> **Indicators from literature:** Income level, Place of residence, Education level of parent/guardian, Family size

Each indicator becomes the basis for one specific objective.

#### Standard Formula for Specific Objectives

> **To [action verb] the [indicator] of [population] in [sector]**

Or more precisely:

> **To determine the effect of [indicator] on [dependent variable] among [population] in [sector]**

#### Worked Example — Full Set of Objectives

**Title:** Economic Factors and Trainee Dropout in TVETs in Nairobi County

**General Objective:**  
To investigate the influence of economic factors on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.

**Specific Objectives:**
1. To determine the effect of **income level** on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
2. To assess the influence of **place of residence** on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
3. To examine the role of **parental/guardian education level** on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
4. To establish the relationship between **family size** and trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.

---

### 2.4 The Alignment Rule (Critical)

All objectives — general and specific — must be **directly connected to the title**. This is called **vertical alignment** or **internal consistency**.

```
TITLE
  │
  ▼
GENERAL OBJECTIVE (mirrors the title)
  │
  ├──► Specific Objective 1 (Indicator 1 of IV)
  ├──► Specific Objective 2 (Indicator 2 of IV)
  ├──► Specific Objective 3 (Indicator 3 of IV)
  └──► Specific Objective 4 (Indicator 4 of IV)
```

> ⚠️ **A specific objective that does not connect back to the title is a misalignment error.**

---

### 2.5 Number of Objectives

| Type | Number |
|---|---|
| General Objective | Exactly **1** |
| Specific Objectives | **3 to 4** |
| **Total** | **4 to 5** |

## 3. STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE: CONSTRUCTING A RESEARCH PROPOSAL TITLE AND OBJECTIVES

### Step 1 — Identify Your Variables

Ask yourself:
- What is the **cause** I want to study? → This is your **Independent Variable**
- What is the **problem or effect** I want to measure? → This is your **Dependent Variable**
- Where or among whom will I conduct the study? → This is your **Sector of Focus**

### Step 2 — Draft the Title

Combine the three components using the formula:

> **[IV] and [DV] in [Sector]**

Count the words. Adjust until you have **10–20 words**.

### Step 3 — Write the General Objective

Restate the title as a purpose statement:

> **To investigate the influence of [IV] on [DV] in [Sector]**

### Step 4 — Review Literature on the IV

Search past studies and identify **3–4 indicators** (sub-components) of your independent variable.

### Step 5 — Write Specific Objectives

Write one specific objective per indicator, using the formula:

> **To [verb] the effect/influence/role of [indicator] on [DV] in [Sector]**

### Step 6 — Check Alignment

Read each specific objective and ask: *Does this connect back to the title?* If yes, it is valid. If no, revise or remove it.

## 4. FULL WORKED EXAMPLE

### Topic Area: TVET Education, Dropout, and Economic Factors

**Title:**  
*Economic Factors and Trainee Dropout in Public TVETs in Nairobi County*  
*(Word count: 12 ✓)*

**Components:**
- IV: Economic Factors
- DV: Trainee Dropout
- Sector: Public TVETs in Nairobi County

**General Objective:**  
To investigate the influence of economic factors on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.

**Indicators of IV from Literature:**
- Income level of the household
- Place of residence (urban vs. peri-urban)
- Education level of parent/guardian
- Family size

**Specific Objectives:**
1. To determine the effect of household income level on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
2. To assess the influence of place of residence on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
3. To examine the role of parental education level on trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.
4. To establish the relationship between family size and trainee dropout in public TVETs in Nairobi County.

## 5. ADDITIONAL EXAMPLES ACROSS SECTORS

### Example A — University Sector

**Title:** *Commercialization and University Funding Model in Public Universities in Kenya*  
*(Word count: 11 ✓)*

- IV: Commercialization
- DV: University Funding Model
- Sector: Public Universities in Kenya

**Specific Objectives:**

4. To establish the contribution of commercialization of research outputs to the university funding model in public universities in Kenya.

## 6. QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST

Use this checklist before submitting your research proposal:

### Title Checklist
- [ ] Title is between 10 and 20 words
- [ ] Independent variable is clearly stated
- [ ] Dependent variable is clearly stated
- [ ] Sector of focus is clearly stated
- [ ] Title is declarative (not a question)
- [ ] Title is specific and unambiguous

### Objectives Checklist
- [ ] Exactly one general objective is stated
- [ ] General objective flows directly from the title
- [ ] General objective uses an appropriate action verb
- [ ] There are 3–4 specific objectives
- [ ] Each specific objective addresses one indicator of the IV
- [ ] Indicators are drawn from literature review
- [ ] All specific objectives connect back to the title
- [ ] Each specific objective uses a different action verb
- [ ] The DV and sector appear consistently across all objectives

## 7. GLOSSARY OF KEY TERMS

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Independent Variable (IV)** | The variable that is manipulated or studied as the cause or influencing factor |
| **Dependent Variable (DV)** | The variable that is measured as the outcome or effect |
| **Sector of Focus** | The specific context, institution, location, or population within which the study is conducted |
| **General Objective** | A single broad statement of the overall purpose of the study, derived from the title |
| **Specific Objective** | A focused, measurable aim addressing one indicator of the independent variable |
| **Indicator** | A sub-component or dimension of a variable, identified through literature review |
| **Vertical Alignment** | The internal consistency between the title, general objective, and specific objectives |
| **Literature Review** | A critical survey of existing research used to identify indicators of variables |
