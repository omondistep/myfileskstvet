# Short Notes: CBET Framework vs. TVETA Framework (Kenya)

## What is a Framework?

A **framework** is a structured system of rules, guidelines, standards, and processes that provides a foundation for designing, implementing, evaluating, and improving a particular activity or system (UNESCO, 2018). In the context of education and training, a framework serves as a **reference point** that ensures consistency, quality, and alignment between different components such as curricula, assessments, qualifications, and regulatory oversight (Kenya National Qualifications Authority, 2015).

Key characteristics of a framework include:
- **Structure** – Organizes components into a logical sequence or hierarchy
- **Standards** – Defines minimum requirements and expectations
- **Guidance** – Provides direction for implementation and decision-making
- **Quality Assurance** – Establishes mechanisms for monitoring and improvement
- **Alignment** – Ensures different parts work together toward common goals

In Kenya's TVET sector, two complementary frameworks operate together: the **CBET Framework** (which governs *how* training is delivered and assessed) and the **TVETA Framework** (which governs *who* provides training and *whether* they meet quality standards).

## i) CBET Framework (Competency-Based Education and Training)

### Definition & Purpose
- A training approach where learners demonstrate **pre-determined levels of competence** (TVET CDACC, n.d.-b).
- Focuses on **mastery of specific competencies** rather than time spent in class (The Star, 2025).
- Produces **competent, employable graduates** responsive to labor market needs (TVET CDACC, 2023).

### Legal & Policy Foundation
- Established under **TVET Act, 2013** (No. 29 of 2013) (Republic of Kenya, 2013).
- **CBETA Standard (TVETS 01:2019)** – provides requirements & guidelines for CBET in Kenya (TVET Authority Board, 2019).

### Key Characteristics
| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Industry-defined standards | Competencies developed with employer input (The Star, 2025) |
| Mastery-based progression | Advance only when competence is demonstrated (The Star, 2025) |
| Practical, hands-on learning | Emphasis on applied skills (The Star, 2025) |
| Flexible assessment | Continuous/modular assessments (The Star, 2025) |
| Link to employability | Competencies tied to occupational standards (The Star, 2025) |

### Key Components in Kenya
- **406 National Occupational Standards** developed by TVET CDACC (TVET CDACC, 2023)
- **12,556 trainers & practitioners** trained on Competency-Based Assessment (TVET CDACC, 2023)
- **Modularized curriculum** – micro-credentials, partial or full qualifications (TVET CDACC, n.d.-a)
- **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)** – certifies informal skills (TVET Authority, 2019)
- **Dual Training Model** – 50% institution / 50% workplace (The Star, 2025)

### Governance Body: TVET CDACC
- Created by TVET Act No. 29 of 2013 (TVET CDACC, n.d.-b)
- Functions: curriculum design, assessment, certification, RPL, foreign qualification recognition

### CBET Curriculum Development (14 Steps)
1. Application to Council
2. Approval of application
3. Appointment of SSAC members
4. Develop Job/Occupation Analysis Chart
5. Verification of Chart
6. Develop Standard Task Analysis Charts
7. Package Occupational Standards
8. Evaluation & validation by SSAC
9. Package competency-based curriculum
10. Evaluation by Council
11. Evaluation by SSAC
12. Validation by stakeholders
13. Forward to Council for approval
14. Approval for Piloting/Implementation

⏱ **Timeframe:** ~30 working days (TVET CDACC, n.d.-b)

### Implementation Status (2023)
- **97 Assessment Centres** registered (TVET CDACC, 2023)
- Pioneer CBET cohort graduated from **Nyandarua National Polytechnic** (2025) (The Star, 2025)

## ii) TVETA Framework (Technical and Vocational Education and Training Authority)

### Definition & Purpose
- A **state corporation** established under TVET Act, 2013 (TVET Authority, n.d.-a)
- Mandate: **regulating and coordinating TVET training** to assure quality and relevance

### Vision & Mission
| Element | Statement |
|---------|-----------|
| **Vision** | Deliver a competent workforce for sustainable development |
| **Mission** | Develop a TVET system that meets national needs and aspirations |

### Core Values
1. Integrity
2. Professionalism
3. Accountability
4. Public Participation
5. Teamwork
6. Efficiency

### Legal Mandate (TVET Act Cap. 210A)
| Function Category | Specific Functions |

| Regulation | Regulate & coordinate TVET training |
| Accreditation | Accredit & inspect programmes/institutions |

| Monitoring & Evaluation | Regular inspection of training & institutions |

| ODeL Oversight | Maintain standards in ODeL |
| Programme Approval | Approve new & review existing programmes |

### Quality Assurance Documents

### Achievements

## Relationship Between CBET and TVETA

### Overview

The relationship between CBET and TVETA is best described as:

> **CBET is the *product/methodology* (the "what" and "how" of training), and TVETA is the *regulator/quality assurer* (the "who" and "whether it meets standards").**

TVETA oversees and enforces the standards that make CBET work legally and effectively in Kenya (TVET Authority, 2019). You cannot implement CBET without TVETA's approval, and TVETA's regulatory role is fulfilled by overseeing frameworks like CBET.

### Key Aspects of the Relationship

#### 1. TVETA is the Regulator; CBET is the Regulated Framework

TVETA is the government body mandated by the **TVET Act of 2013** to regulate all TVET training in Kenya (Republic of Kenya, 2013). CBET is the specific training methodology that TVETA must inspect, accredit, and license.

| Role | TVETA | CBET |
|------|-------|------|
| **Function** | Regulator & quality assurer | Training methodology |
| **Key Question** | "Is the institution ready and compliant?" | "Is the learner competent?" |
| **Output** | License & accreditation | Skilled, competent graduate |

#### 2. TVETA Sets the Standards; CBET Follows Them

TVETA has published specific standards (the **TVETS series**) that provide the legal and quality guidelines for implementing CBET (TVET Authority Board, 2019).

| TVETA Standard | How it relates to CBET |
|:---|:---|
| **TVETS 01:2019 (CBETA)** | The *rulebook* for how CBET must be designed, delivered, and assessed |
| **TVETS 02:2019 (PLAR)** | Regulates how CBET recognizes prior learning (RPL) |
| **TVETS 03:2019 (Trainers)** | Sets minimum qualifications for *trainers* who teach CBET |

**Without TVETA's standards, CBET would have no quality control mechanism.**

#### 3. TVETA is the Inspector; CBET is the Curriculum

- **TVETA** conducts inspections and quality audits to ensure that the CBET curriculum developed by **TVET CDACC** is being taught correctly.
- If a TVET college wants to teach a CBET course (e.g., "Electrical Installation"), they must apply to **TVETA** for accreditation.
- TVETA checks: Do you have the right tools? Are the trainers qualified in CBET methodology? Do you have workshops? If yes, TVETA **licenses** you to teach CBET.

#### 4. They are "Twin Turbines" but Distinct Bodies

As noted in the TVETA document, TVETA and TVET CDACC are the "twin-turbo engines" driving TVET reform in Kenya (TVET Authority, 2019). However, they are legally separate:

| Aspect | **TVETA (Regulator)** | **TVET CDACC (CBET Curricula Developer)** |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Role in CBET** | Enforces the *environment* for CBET | Creates the *content* of CBET |
| **Key Document** | TVETS Standards (01-06) | Occupational Standards & Curricula |
| **Output** | License & Accreditation Certificate | Competency-based curriculum |

### The Circular Relationship (Step-by-Step Process)

Here is how CBET and TVETA work together in practice:

1. **TVET CDACC** develops a **CBET Curriculum** (e.g., Plumbing Level 4) based on industry needs.
2. A college wants to teach that CBET Plumbing course. They **apply to TVETA** for accreditation.
3. **TVETA** inspects the college against the requirements in the CBET curriculum:
   - Appropriate workshops and tools?
   - Trainers qualified in CBET assessment?
   - Adequate learner support systems?
4. **TVETA** issues a **license and accreditation** if requirements are met.
5. The college teaches the **CBET** curriculum using competency-based methods.
6. **TVETA** conducts regular **quality audits** to ensure continued compliance.
7. **TVET CDACC** assesses learners and issues **CBET certificates** to competent graduates.

### Summary Table: CBET vs TVETA Relationship

| Aspect | **CBET Framework** | **TVETA Framework** |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Primary Focus** | Teaching, learning & assessment methodology | Regulation & quality assurance |
| **Key Question** | How should training be delivered/assessed? | Who qualifies to train & do they meet standards? |
| **Governing Body** | TVET CDACC | TVET Authority (TVETA) |
| **Key Documents** | Occupational Standards, CBET Curricula | TVET Act 2013, KEN TVET QAF, TVETS Standards |
| **Output** | Competent graduates with demonstrated skills | Accredited institutions & licensed trainers |
| **Dependency** | Requires TVETA-accredited institutions to operate | Exists to regulate frameworks like CBET |

### Conclusion

> **TVETA is the legal "gatekeeper" and quality inspector for CBET.**
>
> - **Without TVETA:** CBET would be unregulated; anyone could claim to teach it without proper facilities, leading to poor quality graduates.
> - **Without CBET:** TVETA would have no modern, competency-based framework to regulate, and Kenya would remain with outdated theoretical training.

**In short: TVETA ensures that the *promise* of CBET (producing competent, employable workers) is actually delivered by training institutions across Kenya.**
