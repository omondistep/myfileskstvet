# Learning Outcomes — Topic: Inflation
**Diploma in Technical Trainer Education | Unit Code: ED/B/7101**
**Framework: Revised Bloom's Taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001) | All Three Domains**

---

> All learning outcomes are written in SMART format following Mager's (1997) three-part structure:
> **[Condition] + [Performance verb] + [Standard/Criterion]**
>
> **SMART** = Specific · Measurable · Achievable · Relevant · Time-bound

# DOMAIN 1: COGNITIVE DOMAIN
*(Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)*

---

## Level 1 — REMEMBERING

**LO 1.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **define** the term *inflation* and **list** at least four types of inflation (demand-pull, cost-push, built-in, hyperinflation) without reference to notes, as assessed through a written definition exercise completed individually within 10 minutes, achieving 100% accuracy.

**LO 1.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **state** the formula for calculating the inflation rate using the Consumer Price Index (CPI) and **name** at least three goods and services included in Kenya's CPI basket, as verified through a 5-minute oral quiz administered at the close of the session.

---

## Level 2 — UNDERSTANDING

**LO 2.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **explain** the difference between demand-pull and cost-push inflation using at least two real-world examples drawn from the Kenyan economy, as demonstrated in a written paragraph of no fewer than 150 words submitted before the end of the session.

**LO 2.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **summarise** the causes and effects of inflation on households and businesses in Kenya in a structured mind map containing at least six correctly linked nodes, assessed against a provided mind map rubric within the session.

---

## Level 3 — APPLYING

**LO 3.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **calculate** the inflation rate between two given years using the CPI formula — ((CPI Year 2 − CPI Year 1) ÷ CPI Year 1) × 100 — from a provided data set, achieving a minimum score of 70% on a structured calculation exercise completed independently within 20 minutes.

**LO 3.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **apply** the quantity theory of money (MV = PQ) to **demonstrate** how an increase in money supply contributes to inflation, using a provided numerical example and presenting workings in a structured format assessed by the trainer using a step-by-step marking guide.

---

## Level 4 — ANALYSING

**LO 4.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **analyse** Kenya's inflation trends over a five-year period by **examining** provided CPI data, **identifying** at least three contributing factors and explaining the relationship between each factor and the inflation rate, as evidenced in a structured analytical report submitted within 30 minutes.

**LO 4.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **differentiate** between the effects of moderate inflation and hyperinflation on savings, investment, and purchasing power by **constructing** a comparative table with written justifications for each distinction, assessed against a provided rubric within the session.

---

## Level 5 — EVALUATING

**LO 5.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **evaluate** the effectiveness of Kenya's monetary policy in controlling inflation by **critiquing** at least three Central Bank of Kenya interventions (e.g., interest rate adjustments, open market operations, reserve requirements), presenting a justified argument of no fewer than 200 words assessed against an analytical rubric.

**LO 5.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **justify** whether contractionary fiscal policy or monetary policy is the more appropriate tool for reducing demand-pull inflation in Kenya, **defending** their position with reference to at least two economic principles, as demonstrated in a written position paper or 5-minute structured debate assessed by the trainer.

---

## Level 6 — CREATING

**LO 6.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **design** a government anti-inflation policy proposal that recommends two specific interventions — one fiscal and one monetary — **justifying** each with reference to macroeconomic theory and current Kenyan inflation data, presented as a structured one-page policy brief assessed against a provided template.

**LO 6.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will be able to **develop** an original diagram illustrating the inflationary spiral — showing the self-reinforcing cycle between wages, costs, and prices — **producing** an annotated model with a written explanation of at least 250 words, assessed against a marking rubric within a 40-minute task.

# DOMAIN 2: AFFECTIVE DOMAIN
*(Krathwohl, Bloom & Masia, 1964)*

---

## Level 1 — RECEIVING

**LO 1.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **listen attentively** to a 15-minute presentation on the impact of inflation on low-income households in Kenya, demonstrating awareness by **acknowledging** at least two key points raised, as evidenced through active participation in a follow-up think-pair-share activity observed by the trainer.

**LO 1.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **show awareness** of the social consequences of hyperinflation by **attending** to a case study discussion on Zimbabwe's 2008 hyperinflation crisis, as observed by the trainer through active listening and note-taking behaviour recorded on a participation checklist during the session.

---

## Level 2 — RESPONDING

**LO 2.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **participate actively** in a group discussion on how rising inflation affects the cost of living for Kenyan families, **contributing** at least two substantive and relevant points to the discussion, as observed and recorded by the trainer on a participation checklist.

**LO 2.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **respond** to a peer's analysis of inflation trends by **providing** at least one constructive comment using the structured feedback protocol (describe–identify–suggest–connect), as assessed through a peer feedback form completed and submitted during the session.

---

## Level 3 — VALUING

**LO 3.1**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **demonstrate commitment** to using accurate and current inflation data in economic analysis by **consistently citing** credible sources (e.g., Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, Central Bank of Kenya, World Bank) in all submitted assignments, as assessed through a source credibility checklist applied to three pieces of coursework.

**LO 3.2**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **show appreciation** for the disproportionate impact of inflation on vulnerable populations by **advocating** for at least one pro-poor inflation mitigation policy in a written position paper of no fewer than 300 words, justifying their stance with reference to inflation data and economic theory, assessed against a provided rubric.

---

## Level 4 — ORGANISING

**LO 4.1**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **formulate** a personal value position that **reconciles** the tension between controlling inflation and maintaining economic growth, presenting their position in a 300-word reflective essay that integrates both values in the context of Kenya's macroeconomic policy, assessed against a reflective writing rubric.

**LO 4.2**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **prioritise** transparency and ethical responsibility in economic reporting by **integrating** these values consistently into all three submitted inflation analysis assignments, as evidenced by accurate data attribution, honest interpretation of trends, and zero instances of data misrepresentation confirmed through trainer review.

---

## Level 5 — CHARACTERISING

**LO 5.1**
By the end of the course, the learner will **consistently demonstrate** professional integrity in economic analysis by **habitually applying** ethical standards when interpreting and presenting inflation data across all coursework and practical assessments, as evidenced by zero instances of data misrepresentation in the portfolio and confirmed through trainer observation records spanning the full unit.

**LO 5.2**
By the end of the course, the learner will **embody** a commitment to evidence-based economic reasoning by **independently seeking** current inflation data from credible sources (KNBS, CBK, IMF) in all analytical tasks without prompting, as observed and recorded across a minimum of five assessed activities throughout the unit.

# DOMAIN 3: PSYCHOMOTOR DOMAIN
*(Simpson, 1972)*

---

## Level 1 — PERCEPTION

**LO 1.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **detect** the key structural features of a correctly constructed inflation rate table — including appropriate column headings, CPI values, base year, and percentage change — by **observing** two sample tables (one correct, one flawed) and **identifying** at least three errors in the flawed version, as assessed through a written observation checklist completed within 10 minutes.

**LO 1.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **recognise** the visual cues that distinguish an accurately scaled inflation trend graph from a misleading one by **attending** to a trainer-led demonstration, **identifying** at least two formatting or scaling errors in a provided sample graph, as verified through a short written response submitted before the end of the session.

---

## Level 2 — SET

**LO 2.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **prepare** a workstation for an inflation data analysis task by **setting up** a correctly formatted Excel spreadsheet — including labelled columns for Year, CPI, and Inflation Rate — with appropriate units and a data entry area ready before data input begins, as verified by the trainer against a setup checklist within 5 minutes.

**LO 2.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **demonstrate readiness** to construct an inflation trend graph by **organising** all required materials (graph paper, pencil, ruler, data sheet) and **stating** the variables to be plotted on each axis before beginning the task, as observed and confirmed by the trainer at the start of the practical activity.

---

## Level 3 — GUIDED RESPONSE

**LO 3.1**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **follow** the trainer's step-by-step instructions to **enter** annual CPI data into Excel and **replicate** the construction of a basic inflation rate line graph for Kenya (2018–2023), completing the guided task within 20 minutes with trainer support available, as assessed through direct observation using a task completion checklist.

**LO 3.2**
By the end of the lesson, the learner will **attempt** to draw a demand-pull inflation diagram (showing a rightward shift of the AD curve) by **copying** a model demonstrated by the trainer, producing a hand-drawn version with correctly labelled axes (Price Level and Real GDP), the original and shifted AD curves, and the resulting price level change, assessed against a diagram elements checklist.

---

## Level 4 — MECHANISM

**LO 4.1**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **independently construct** a line graph showing Kenya's annual inflation rate over five years using provided CPI data — **without** step-by-step instructions — **producing** a correctly labelled graph with appropriate scale, title, axis labels, and data points, assessed against a graph construction checklist within 20 minutes.

**LO 4.2**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **independently operate** a spreadsheet application to **calculate** annual inflation rates from a provided CPI data set using the correct formula, **producing** a formatted worksheet with correct formula inputs, calculated values, and a summary table, completed within 25 minutes and assessed by the trainer against an output checklist.

---

## Level 5 — COMPLEX OVERT RESPONSE

**LO 5.1**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **accurately and fluently construct** a fully labelled cost-push inflation diagram — showing a leftward shift of the AS curve with correctly labelled axes, original and new equilibrium points, and annotated price and output effects — **without reference to notes**, achieving a minimum score of 80% on a diagram accuracy checklist under timed conditions (15 minutes).

**LO 5.2**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **precisely and smoothly execute** the full process of calculating the real interest rate from nominal interest rate and inflation data using the Fisher equation (r ≈ i − π), **producing** a correctly formatted solution with all workings shown and achieving 100% accuracy on a structured task completed independently within 20 minutes.

---

## Level 6 — ADAPTATION

**LO 6.1**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **adapt** a standard inflation data presentation to suit two different audiences — a Central Bank policy brief and a community consumer awareness leaflet — by **modifying** the format, language, scale, and visual representation accordingly, with both outputs assessed against audience-specific rubrics submitted within one week.

**LO 6.2**
By the end of the unit, the learner will **adjust** their Excel-based inflation analysis model when given a revised data set that incorporates food and fuel price shocks, **reorganising** the spreadsheet structure and **recalculating** inflation figures to reflect the new data, completed within 30 minutes and assessed against a revised output checklist.

---

## Level 7 — ORIGINATION

**LO 7.1**
By the end of the course, the learner will **design** an original inflation monitoring dashboard in Excel — **creating** a layout, chart types, and formula structure of their own choosing — that tracks and visualises Kenya's monthly CPI, annual inflation rate, and core inflation trends in a clear and professional format, assessed against a self-developed rubric agreed with the trainer before submission.

**LO 7.2**
By the end of the course, the learner will **devise** an original method for communicating the relationship between inflation and purchasing power to a non-specialist audience, **producing** a novel infographic or data visualisation that presents key trends accessibly and accurately, assessed through a peer and trainer review panel against criteria of accuracy, clarity, and originality.
