## Activity 1 — What Is CBET and Why Does It Matter? (20 min)

### Why This Activity Matters
Most trainee trainers have been educated in a content-delivery model — a teacher talks, learners listen, learners are tested on what they heard. CBET is a fundamentally different philosophy. Before you can teach within it, you need to understand *why* it exists and what problem it was designed to solve. This activity surfaces your existing assumptions so they can be examined, not just replaced.

### Step 1 — Individual Reflection (5 min)
Write brief answers in your notebook. Do not discuss yet — your first instinct matters here.

1. Think of a time you learned to do something well — a skill, a craft, a procedure. How did you learn it? Was it from a lecture or from doing?
2. What does it mean to say someone is "competent" at their job? Is competence the same as passing an exam?
3. If you were an employer hiring a TVET graduate, what evidence would convince you they were ready to work?

### Step 2 — Pair Discussion (5 min)
Share your answers with a partner. Identify: (a) one assumption you both share, and (b) one point where you see things differently.

### Step 3 — Whole-Group Debrief (10 min)
The facilitator draws out key ideas. Together, construct a working definition of CBET on the board.

**Anchor your definition to TVETA (2020):**
> CBET requires that training be industry-led, outcome-focused, and evidence-based. Certification is granted on demonstrated competence, not seat time.

**Critical thinking prompt:** TVETA (2020) states that the shift to CBET was driven by a "mismatch between skills acquired through training and skills needed by industry." What does this tell us about the failure of the previous system? Who bears the cost of that mismatch — the graduate, the employer, or society?

**Connect to UNESCO (2022):** UNESCO's TVET Strategy 2022–2029 identifies three strategic priorities:
1. Develop skills for all individuals to *learn, work, and live*
2. Develop skills for *inclusive and sustainable economies*
3. Develop skills for *inclusive and peaceful societies*

Kenya's CBET reforms are not isolated — they are part of a global repositioning of TVET as a pathway for human development, not just job training. UNESCO (2022) notes that 267 million young people globally are not in employment, education, or training. CBET, done well, is a response to that crisis.

## Activity 2 — Anatomy of an Occupational Standard (40 min)

### Why This Activity Matters
The Occupational Standard is the most important document in CBET — it is the authoritative definition of what competence looks like in a specific occupation. Every lesson plan, every assessment, every piece of evidence you collect as a trainer must trace back to the OS. If you cannot read an OS fluently, you cannot design or deliver CBET training.

### Background: Structure of the Business Manager OS (TVET CDACC, 2025)

The OS is organised into three tiers, reflecting the structure of the Kenya National Qualifications Framework (KNQF):

| Tier | Purpose | Examples from Business Manager OS |
|------|---------|----------------------------------|
| **Basic Units** | Generic workplace competencies applicable across all occupations | Apply Digital Literacy (BUS/OS/BM/BC/01/6/MA); Apply Work Ethics and Practices; Apply Entrepreneurial Skills |
| **Common Units** | Sector-wide competencies shared across business occupations | Apply Business Communication (BUS/OS/BM/CC/01/5/MA); Apply Management Skills; Apply Financial Accounting Skills; Conduct Research Project |
| **Core Units** | Occupation-specific competencies that define the Business Manager role | Perform Sales Activities; Manage Marketing Activities; Perform Human Resource Activities; Manage Business Risks; Carry out Accounting Operations |

Each unit follows this internal structure:

| Component | What it tells you | Why it matters for training |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------------|
| **Unit Code** | Unique identifier (e.g. BUS/OS/BM/CC/01/5/MA) | Links your lesson plan to the OS — always cite it |
| **Unit Description** | Scope and purpose of the competence | Tells you the *why* behind the unit |
| **Elements** | The main tasks within the unit (usually 3–6) | Each element becomes a cluster of lessons |
| **Performance Criteria** | The standard to which each task must be performed | These are your learning outcomes — derive them directly |
| **Range** | The contexts, conditions, and variables that apply | Tells you the breadth of situations learners must be prepared for |
| **Evidence Requirements** | What proof of competence is needed | Drives your assessment design |

**Critical thinking prompt:** The OS was developed by industry through Sector Skills Committees (TVET CDACC, 2025). This means employers — not educators — defined what competence looks like. What are the advantages of this approach? What risks does it carry? (Consider: what happens if industry standards lag behind technological change? What if the industry has low standards?)

### Task 2a — OS Dissection (20 min)

Open the Business Manager OS to the unit **Apply Business Communication** (BUS/OS/BM/CC/01/5/MA).

Read the unit carefully. Then complete this analysis table in your notebook:

| OS Component | What you found in this unit | Your interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Unit description | Write it out | What is the core purpose of this unit in one sentence? |
| Number of elements | Count them | Which element do you think is most important for a TVET graduate? Why? |
| One performance criterion (write in full) | Copy it exactly | Is this criterion observable? How would you know if a learner met it? |
| One range variable | Copy it | How does this range variable affect how you would design a training activity? |
| Evidence requirement | State what is required | What type of evidence is this — product, process, or testimony? |

**Note on evidence types (TVETA, 2020):**
- **Product evidence** — something the learner produces (a written report, a completed form, a designed document)
- **Process evidence** — something the trainer observes the learner doing (a demonstration, a role play, a practical task)
- **Testimony evidence** — confirmation from a third party (supervisor statement, peer witness, RPL declaration)

CBET requires a combination of evidence types because no single type can confirm competence across all performance criteria.

### Task 2b — Group Comparison (10 min)

In groups of 3, compare your completed tables. Discuss:

1. Did you interpret the same performance criterion the same way? If not, what does that tell you about the precision of OS language?
2. Which performance criteria are directly and easily observable? Which require more creative evidence collection?
3. One group member argues: *"I could just give learners a written test on business communication and that would cover all the performance criteria."* How would you respond? Reference TVETA (2020).

### Task 2c — Whole-Group Share (10 min)

Each group shares one insight. The facilitator records responses under two columns:

**Directly observable** | **Requires creative evidence design**

Discuss: What does the distribution of responses tell us about the challenge of CBET assessment?

## Activity 3 — From OS to Curriculum: Constructive Alignment in Practice (30 min)

### Why This Activity Matters
The curriculum is the bridge between the OS (what industry requires) and the classroom (what the trainer delivers). A well-designed curriculum is *constructively aligned* — every learning outcome traces to an OS performance criterion, every activity develops that outcome, and every assessment generates the evidence the OS requires. A poorly designed curriculum breaks this chain, producing learners who can pass assessments but cannot perform in the workplace.

Biggs & Tang (2022) call this *constructive alignment*: the principle that intended learning outcomes, teaching and learning activities, and assessment tasks must all point at the same target. In CBET, this alignment is mandated by TVETA — but it only works if the trainer understands and uses the documents correctly.

### Background: Structure of the Business Management Curriculum (TVET CDACC, 2025)

The curriculum is organised into five modules. Each module contains units of learning with:
- Learning outcomes (derived from OS performance criteria)
- Suggested delivery methods
- Training/learning resources
- Assessment methods
- Duration in hours (credit factor)

For example, *Business Communication* (Module I) is allocated 80 hours with a credit factor of 8.

Find the curriculum unit for *Apply Business Communication* in the Business Management Curriculum. Working individually, complete this mapping table for **at least three** performance criteria:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | |
| | | | | |

**Alignment test:** For each row, ask — if a learner completes the assessment method listed, will they have generated the evidence the OS performance criterion requires? If yes, the system is aligned. If no, there is a gap.

### Task 3b — Alignment Discussion (10 min)

With a partner, discuss:

1. Did you find any gaps between the OS performance criteria and the curriculum learning outcomes? Describe one specifically.
2. Did you find any assessment methods that would not generate sufficient TVETA evidence? What would you replace them with?
3. Biggs & Tang (2022) warn that misalignment produces a "hidden curriculum" — learners learn to pass assessments rather than develop genuine competence. Can you identify any risk of this in the curriculum you just analysed?

**Critical thinking prompt:** The curriculum was developed by TVET CDACC (2025) and the OS by TVET CDACC (2025) — they come from the same institution. Does that guarantee alignment? What factors might cause drift between the two documents over time?

## Activity 4 — Evidence Requirements: Designing for Proof (20 min)

### Why This Activity Matters
In CBET, assessment is not about testing knowledge — it is about collecting *sufficient, valid, and authentic* evidence that a learner can perform a competence to the required standard. As a trainer, you are responsible for designing assessment tasks that generate this evidence. If your evidence is insufficient, TVETA will not certify the learner.

### Task 4a — Evidence Audit (15 min)

For the unit *Apply Business Communication*, complete this evidence planning table. For each element, identify what evidence you would collect and how:

| Element | Product Evidence (what learner produces) | Process Evidence (what trainer observes) | Testimony Evidence (third-party confirmation) | Sufficient for TVETA? |
|---------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Element 1 | | | | |
| Element 2 | | | | |
| Element 3 | | | | |

**Guidance:** TVETA (2020) requires evidence to be:
- **Valid** — it actually measures the performance criterion
- **Sufficient** — enough evidence to confirm consistent competence, not a one-off performance
- **Authentic** — genuinely produced by the learner
- **Current** — reflects the learner's present competence, not past learning

### Task 4b — Written Reflection (5 min)

Write one paragraph (~100 words) responding to:

> *Why does CBET require multiple types of evidence rather than a single written test? What would be lost if assessment were reduced to a written examination only?*

Your paragraph must reference both TVETA (2020) and UNESCO (2022). UNESCO (2022) argues that TVET must produce graduates who can navigate "digital, green and inclusive economies" — consider whether a written test alone can confirm readiness for that.

## CAT 1 — Document Analysis Report (10%)

### Purpose
This assessment tests your ability to read, interpret, and critically analyse the foundational documents of CBET. It is the evidence for Element 1: Interpret Training Standards.

### What to Submit
A structured written report of **600–800 words** (excluding tables and references) that:

**Section 1 — Unit Identification**
State the unit code, title, tier (basic/common/core), and a brief description of the unit you have chosen to analyse. Explain why this unit is important in the Business Manager qualification.

**Section 2 — Structural Analysis**
List all elements of the unit. For each element, list the performance criteria. Present this as a numbered list or table. Comment on the precision of the language — are the criteria specific enough to be assessed? Are any ambiguous?

**Section 3 — Curriculum Mapping**
Present your mapping table (OS performance criteria → curriculum learning outcomes → assessment methods). Evaluate the alignment: where is it strong? Where are the gaps? Reference Biggs & Tang (2022) on constructive alignment.

**Section 4 — Evidence Requirements**
Explain what types of evidence (product, process, testimony) are required for this unit and how a trainer would collect them. Reference TVETA (2020) on the validity, sufficiency, authenticity, and currency of evidence.

**Section 5 — Contextualisation**
Connect your chosen unit to:
- TVETA (2020) — how does this unit fit within the CBET framework and Kenya's qualification structure?
- UNESCO (2022) — which of the three strategic priorities does this unit serve? Explain the connection specifically, not generically.

**Appendix:** Attach annotated photocopies or screenshots of the OS and curriculum extracts you analysed. Your annotations must be visible.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Marks |
